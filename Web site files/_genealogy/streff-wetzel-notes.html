Paul Albert Streff 1853-1940

Born in Port Washington, Wisconsin. Left for Loda Illinois in 1875 where he stayed on a farm until 1878. He then moved to Chicago where he worked for a year in the butcher shop of his brother-in-law Joe Peerling. He then went to work for Marshall Field in wholesale furs, traveling the midwest. In 1897 opened general (dry goods) store on Webster and Racine in Chicago. 1907, sold the store and bought a six-flat building at 5250 Kenmore where he lived until 1924 when he bought the house at 1720 Estes avenue, his final residence.

The Wetzels' Journey to America

On November 18, 1855, the ship "St. Nicholas" sailed from La Havre, France. The cost of passage was 1510 francs. Aboard this vessel was Elizabeth Wetzel, nee Wetzel, age 31 (born April 1818). Accompanying her was her brother Peter Wetzel, age 27, who was brought to America to assist Elizabeth with her nine children on the sailing vessel.

Her husband, Nicholas Wetzel, (1808-1894) had come to America and was settled in Waukegan, Illinois. He had a new house and garden ready when they arrived.

Nicholas was a cabinet maker - all the furniture was hand made. Emily Michaels and Ann Sessler had some of the pieces.

The children were:

  1. Madelena, age 16 (married name Keller)
  2. Marie, age 14 (married name Martin)
  3. Emma, age 13 (married name Mohrman)
  4. Ann Elisa, age 11 (married name Sessler)
  5. Catherine, age 9 (married name Delany)
  6. George, age 7
  7. Elizabeth, age 5 (married name Michaels)
  8. Nicholas, age 4
  9. Celestine, age 2 (married name Streff)

Two more children, Charles and Adam were born in Waukegan. Adam drowned in a cistern at age 4.
